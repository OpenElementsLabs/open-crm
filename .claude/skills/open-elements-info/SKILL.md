# Open Elements Company Information

## Overview

Open Elements GmbH is a German IT company focusing on open-source software (OSS) and Java.
By specializing in Java-based OSS, we provide expert advice and support in developing and using open-source software.
We are members of the leading committees and foundations on these topics and actively contribute to the further development of critical open-source systems in the Java ecosystem.

Open Elements sustainably reinvests income from Support & Care into OSS projects.

The company motto is "Open Source made right."

## Positioning — "The Address for Open Source"

Today, every large German enterprise runs software that belongs to no one — open-source software (OSS) created by volunteers, with no vendor, no contract, and no point of contact when something goes wrong. That same software is the foundation of every banking, insurance, and industrial IT stack.

Open Elements is the **contractual partner** that takes responsibility for these "gifted tools" — the company enterprises can turn to when they need a warranty, maintenance, and an accountable point of contact for OSS components.

Two facts that make this positioning concrete:

- **Founding member of the Eclipse Adoptium Working Group** — the foundation group behind Temurin, the most widely used Java runtime worldwide.
- **One of the two companies worldwide** authorized to provide hardened container images for the German federal administration. Java applications of the German government will, in the future, run on a base produced by Open Elements.

What Open Elements already delivers today becomes an EU obligation by end of 2027: the **Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)** will require every manufacturer of a commercial product to take responsibility for all software components it contains — including the open-source building blocks it did not write itself. Thanks to years of upfront work in the relevant OSS foundations — often voluntary, long before there was a business case — Open Elements is one of the few European companies that can credibly act as a contractual partner for exactly these building blocks.

## Foundation Memberships and Roles

- **Eclipse Foundation**: Contributing Member — Hendrik Ebbers is a member of the Board of Directors (Europe's largest OSS foundation).
- **Eclipse Adoptium**: Participant Member — Hendrik Ebbers is a founding member of Eclipse Adoptium (formerly on the AdoptOpenJDK TSC).
- **JakartaEE**: Participant Member.
- **Open Regulatory Compliance Working Group (ORC WG)**: Founding Member and Participant Member (https://orcwg.org). Open Elements actively contributes to a practical implementation of the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) rules for open-source software and industry. Initially Hendrik Ebbers was a regular participant in the WG meetings; Sebastian Tiemann has since taken over and is especially active in the CRA Attestation Working Group (https://github.com/orcwg/cra-attestations).
- **Linux Foundation**: The world's most influential open-source foundation, structured into sectoral sub-foundations (analogous to DIN's separate committees for automotive, construction, medical technology, etc.). Open Elements is active in two of these:
  - **LFDT (Linux Foundation Decentralized Trust)** — the sector for digital trust technologies: blockchain, digital identity, secure supply chains. These are the exact topics currently being regulated by the EU (eIDAS, EU Digital Identity Wallet). Through Hendrik Ebbers' position as **Vice Chair of LFDT's highest technical body** (Technical Advisory Board), Open Elements is among the handful of actors worldwide that co-determine the technical roadmap of this domain.
  - **AAIF (Agentic AI Foundation)** — the sector for standards around AI agents, founded in late 2025. Covers the next generation of AI in which software systems do not just answer questions but make decisions and execute transactions autonomously. Hendrik Ebbers works in AAIF working groups together with Anthropic, OpenAI, and Block on open standards for **Agentic Commerce** and **Agentic Trust** — i.e. how AI agents can securely transact and trust each other. This is not speculative: Mastercard, Visa, Google, and Stripe are already running productive experiments with similar approaches.
  - **Hiero project**: Hendrik Ebbers is Technical Steering Committee (TSC) Chair.
- **Apache Maven**: Sponsor and active contributor.
- **BVMW**: Member (German Association for Small/Medium Businesses).
- **iJUG e.V.**: Member (German Java User Groups umbrella organization).
